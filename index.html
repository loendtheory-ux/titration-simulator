Assumptions & Approximations

▼
  • Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation: Used in buffer regions where both weak acid/base and its conjugate are present. Valid when pH is within ±1 of pKa (or pKb).
  • Equivalence Point pH ≠ 7: For weak acid–strong base titrations, the equivalence point pH > 7 due to hydrolysis of the conjugate base. For weak base–strong acid, pH < 7 due to conjugate acid hydrolysis.
  • Activity vs. Concentration: We use molar concentrations instead of activities for simplicity at the high school level.
  • Temperature: All calculations assume 25°C (298 K) where Kw = 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴.
  • Dilution Effects: Volume changes from mixing are accounted for in all calculations.
  • Simplifying Assumptions: We use approximations (like ignoring autoionization of water when [H⁺] or [OH⁻] is sufficiently large) that are standard in introductory chemistry.
  • pH Indicators: Indicator transition ranges are approximate. Color changes occur gradually over the pH range shown, not instantly.
